Welcome, Fellow. This package is your companion for putting the CHL AI Agent System to work. The system is a set of AI collaborators — one for each phase of the Culture Hack Method — that help you build a point of view, listen to the narrative space, understand it, recode it, and design and evaluate your hack. Think of the agents as skilled partners rather than decision-makers: they surface patterns, draft, and organise, but community knowledge, political judgment, and creative vision stay firmly with you. Everything here is grounded in the method's commitment to justice and an ontological shift — narratives that change how we relate to the world, never the status quo dressed in progressive language — and in a simple test you'll return to again and again: if you showed this to the community, would they recognise themselves?

The documentation is arranged to move with you. Start with this **Introduction**, then the **Install** guide (how to set each agent up as its own project), then a short **introduction to each agent** in the order you'll meet them — what it does, how to work with it, and what goes in and comes out. You don't need to read it all at once: install the first agents, and let Agent 00 guide you to whatever you need next. *Welcome to the work of making the radical common sense.*

## 1. What it is

The CHL AI Agent System is a set of AI collaborators that walk you through the **Culture Hack Method**, one phase at a time. Each agent is an expert in a single phase of narrative-led systems change. You move through them in sequence, passing the output of one agent into the next.

```
Ask  →  Listen  →  Understand  →  Recode  →  Hack + Evaluate
01     02a→02b       03            04          05a + 05b
```

And at the entrance: **Agent 00 · Q&A** — ask it anything about the method or where to go next.

### The eight agents at a glance

| Agent | Phase | What it does | Produces → passes to |
| ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **00 · Q&A** | Entry point | Answers questions, defines concepts, routes you to the right agent. Doesn't produce phase outputs. | Answers + navigation |
| **01 · Ask** | Ask | Builds your collective **Point of View** (POV): who you are, the narrative problem, the shift you seek, your theory of change. | Short POV → 02a |
| **02a · Listen** | Listen (design) | Turns your POV into a **Listening Model** and a Big Listening Inquiry — the research plan. | Big Listening Inquiry → 02b |
| **02b · Research** | Listen (do) | Runs the **Big Listening** research and processes Small Listening data. | Narrative Report + CSV Database + ANP Source Library → 03 |
| **03 · Understand** | Understand | Analyses communities and narratives — ANP deep-dive, frame analysis, the Justice/Ontology map, the Window of Discourse, Acupuncture Points. | Community Analysis Report + Narrative Map (incl. Narrative Intentions + Window of Discourse) → 04 |
| **04 · Recode** | Recode | **Decodes** the dominant narrative and **recodes** it into a new message, using the eight lenses, plus audience and narrative strategy. | Narrative Strategy + Recoded Narrative (with Message) + Audience Profile(s) → 05a |
| **05a · Hack** | Hack | Turns the message into a concrete intervention via the **Hacking Tree** (Media, Meme, Tone, Hack: Space/Scale/Moment) + a Logistics Plan. | Hacking Tree + Logistics Plan → 05b |
| **05b · Impact** | Evaluate + Iterate | Documents and **evaluates** the hack (three-level impact), builds the Hack Database and a Case Study, and routes the next cycle. | Case Study + updated POV → 02a or 04 |

An agent is **a collaborator, not a decision-maker.** Community knowledge, political judgment, and creative vision stay with you.

> **A note on numbering.** Hack and Evaluate are one phase — Phase 5 — split across two agents: **05a · Hack** designs the intervention, and **05b · Impact** evaluates it and routes the next cycle. Evaluate is nested inside the Hack phase, not a separate sixth phase.

## 2. The ethics behind it

The system carries a clear political stance and a discipline for responsible AI use. Both are built into every agent.

**Political stance — justice and onto-shift.** The agents support narratives that advance justice and an *ontological shift* — a fundamental change in how we relate to the world, centring plural ways of knowing (Indigenous epistemologies, community knowledge, embodied and relational understanding). They will not produce outputs that prop up the status quo, even when it's dressed in progressive language. If an output drifts from this, that's your signal to push back — your judgment takes precedence.

**Responsible AI use — the 4Ds.** Every agent coaches you toward healthy practice:

- **Delegation** — let AI find patterns across large data; keep community interviews, cultural nuance, and ethical calls human.
- **Description** — give the AI real context: which community, how it talks, the scope, the output you want.
- **Discernment** — apply the **community test**: *if you showed this to the community, would they recognise themselves?* If not, treat it as a starting point, not a conclusion.
- **Diligence** — be transparent with communities that you're using AI, protect consent and privacy, and verify findings through real engagement.

**Human-in-the-loop by design.** Certain moments must stay with people: developing the POV (01), positioning communities on the map — especially spotting "Ontofake" (03), approving the exact recoded message (04), the creative hack decisions (05a), and the three closing reflection questions on self, community, and planet (05b). These aren't optimisation targets; they're intentional.

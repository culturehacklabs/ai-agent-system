## What This Phase Is

Ask is the entry point of the Culture Hack Method. Its purpose is to establish the **Point of View (POV)** — the collective statement that sets the foundation for the entire narrative intervention.

The POV is not an organisational mission statement or a summary of the problem a collective works on. It names a **narrative problem**: a specific cultural construct or dominant frame that is sustaining the conditions the collective wants to change — and a theory of how shifting that narrative will contribute to systems change.

Without a clear POV, the hack lacks direction. It becomes a communications exercise rather than a strategic intervention. The POV is the north star that all subsequent phases orient toward: listening, analysis, strategy, and intervention.

---

## Key Concepts

**Point of View (POV)**
A collective statement with four parts: who we are, what we observe in the narrative landscape, what we want to shift, and how we believe that shift will happen. It names a narrative problem, not just a material condition.

*The difference:* "Housing is unaffordable for working families" is a material condition. "The dominant narrative frames housing as an investment asset rather than a human right — making dispossession feel natural and inevitable" is a narrative problem.

**Collective Identity**
The POV is written in the first-person plural: "We are…". It names the group, movement, or collective undertaking the hack — not individual practitioners. The identity should be grounded in the practitioner's relationship to the narrative space they want to enter.

**Narrative Observation**
What the collective sees in the current narrative landscape. This is an observation about a dominant cultural construct, frame, or story — not a description of what is materially wrong. It answers: *What story is being told that sustains the conditions we want to change? What does that story make visible — and what does it hide?*

**Vision**
What would become possible if the narrative shifted. Not a programme or policy goal — a statement of what different sense-making would open up.

**Theory of Change**
How shifting this narrative contributes to the larger transformation the collective seeks. The bridge between the POV and the hack. It answers: *Why does narrative matter here? What changes when the story changes?*

**Short POV**
A 1–3 sentence distillation of the Extended POV. This is the direct handoff to Agent 02a. It must be specific enough to generate a research question — not a general statement of values or concern.

**Five Pathways to Regenerative Futures** *(Rhizome Fellowship context only)*
If working within the Rhizome Fellowship, the POV connects to one of five thematic anchors:
1. Restoring Justice, Restoring Life
2. Economies of Regeneration
3. Land Back to Right Relationship
4. Bioregional Community Governance
5. Healing the Spirit, Healing the Earth

If working outside the Fellowship, skip this entirely. The POV stands on its own.

## The POV Format

**Four-part structure:**
> *"We are [collective identity] / we see that [narrative observation] / Therefore we want [vision] / To do this, we must [theory of change]"*

**Two output formats:**

| Format | Length | Purpose |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Extended POV | One full paragraph | Detailed reference; internal document |
| Short POV | 1–3 sentences | Handoff to Agent 02a; orients the research question |

The Short POV distils the narrative problem and theory of change. It should be specific enough that someone reading it knows *exactly* what narrative space is being hacked and *why*.

---

## Documentation Questions

Work through these blocks in order. In Hacking Mode, they can be asked together. In Learning Mode, one block at a time.

**Block 1 — Identity**
- Who are the people or collective behind this hack? How do you describe yourselves?
- What is your relationship to the narrative space you want to intervene in? Are you inside it, adjacent to it, or challenging it from outside?

**Block 2 — Narrative Observation**
- What narrative, frame, or cultural construct are you challenging?
- What does the current dominant narrative make visible — and what does it hide or make seem unthinkable?
- How does this narrative sustain the conditions you want to change?

*Prompt if the answer describes a material condition rather than a narrative:* "What story justifies or naturalises that condition? What does the dominant frame say about why things are this way — and why they should stay this way?"

**Block 3 — Vision**
- What would shift if the narrative changed? What becomes possible?
- What larger transformation is this connected to?

**Block 4 — Theory of Change**
- How does changing this narrative contribute to the larger transformation you seek?
- What is your role in that process?

**Optional — Timeline** *(imagination exercise, not a fixed plan)*
- If this narrative shift happened over the next few years, what might look different?
- Where could this work lead in the longer term?

---

## Pressure-Test Questions

Before producing the final POV, check:

- Does the narrative observation name a **frame or cultural construct** — not just a social problem?
- Is the collective identity **plural and grounded** — not a generic label?
- Is the theory of change a **narrative theory** — not a policy or programme theory?
- Is the Short POV **specific enough** to generate a focused research question for Agent 02a?

---

## What Comes Before / After

**Before:** This is the entry point. Practitioners may arrive with a general area of concern, an existing project, a draft POV, or a workshop transcript. The agent helps them sharpen and formalise it.

**After:** The Short POV passes to **Agent 02a (Listening Model)**, which transforms it into a research question and listening parameters.

**On iteration:** In a second or later cycle, the POV is refined — not rebuilt. New findings from the hack may sharpen the narrative observation or theory of change. The core identity and vision typically remain stable. The agent helps the practitioner identify *what specifically* to update and *why*, based on what the previous cycle revealed.

## What Logistics Is

Logistics is the reality check. The Hacking Tree designs the intervention; Logistics asks whether the team can actually make it happen — and what needs to be in place before, during, and after.

Two tracks are available:

**Lean logistics ⚡** — for teams that need to move quickly or are in early planning. Covers the essentials: timeline, roles, minimum budget, and documentation baseline. Fast to complete; enough to get started.

**Full logistics 🔍** — for teams preparing a serious implementation, a fellowship deliverable, or a multi-phase campaign. Covers all sub-sections in depth including capacity assessment, external needs, budget breakdown, documentation plan, and monitoring with response protocols.

Ask the practitioner which track fits their situation before proceeding. If unsure, lean is the better default — it can always be expanded.

**On documentation:** This section captures the *plan* — what will be documented, how, and what metrics tracked during launch. The full processing and evaluation of that evidence belongs to Agent 05b.

## Prototyping Philosophy

Culture hacking is iterative by nature. The goal is not a perfect intervention — it is one that is good enough to launch and learn from. A hack that ships and produces unexpected results teaches more than one that never launches.

Before finalising the logistics plan, one question worth asking: *is there a small version of this hack we could test with a handful of people from the target audience before full launch?* Not a gate — a choice. Testing a meme in a conversation, running a version of the workshop with 10 people, or sharing the visual with a trusted community member costs little and can sharpen everything.

The philosophy: wax on, wax off. Each cycle teaches the next one.

# KB: Recode — Actions A: Audience Profile
**Agent 04 · CHL AI Agent System**
*Load when defining the audience or producing an Audience Profile.*

---

## What This Output Is

The Audience Profile translates the analytical community picture from Agent 03 into a concrete portrait of the people the hack needs to reach. It describes the audience as specific people — not a demographic category, not a community label. The profile should be concrete enough that the practitioner can picture a real person: where they live, what they hear about this issue, what they feel but don't say, what they do.

This specificity is what makes creative and strategic decisions possible. A hack is designed for people, not for concepts.

It is an **optional but recommended output** — particularly useful when:
- The audience is not yet clearly defined
- The practitioner is designing a creative intervention and needs to feel who they are talking to
- Multiple audience segments need to be distinguished before decode/recode begins

A profile is produced per audience segment. If two profiles are produced, the agent should check for coherence before proceeding to decode/recode.

---

## Audience Coherence Check

Before producing profiles or proceeding to decode, confirm:

- Do both audiences share enough common ground to be reached by a single message and hack?
- Or do they require different messages, different formats, different moments?

**If incompatible:** The practitioner must choose one audience for this hack, or plan two separate interventions — each with its own decode/recode and its own entry in the Narrative Strategy. Name this clearly. It is not a failure — it is strategic clarity.

**Coherence test questions:**
- Would the same message resonate with both audiences?
- Would the same media format reach both?
- Would the emotional tone required for one alienate the other?
- Are these audiences part of the same community network, or in different worlds?

## Audience Profile Template

```
## Audience Profile: [Name or descriptor]

**Community of origin:** [Which narrative community from Agent 03 does this audience sit within — or between?]
**Relationship to practitioner:** [How does the collective have access to or connection with this audience?]
**Catalytic potential:** [If this audience shifts, what else could move? Who do they influence?]

### Who They Are
- Age range: [approximate]
- Location: [geographic or platform context]
- Gender / identity markers: [relevant dimensions]
- Social/political position: [brief description]
- Relationship to the issue: [how close or distant are they to the narrative space being hacked?]

### What They Hear
[What narratives, messages, or frames does this audience regularly encounter about the topic?
What are they told — by media, institutions, community, family?
This is where the dominant narrative lives for them.]

### What They See
[What does their daily environment show them about this topic?
What images, symbols, behaviours, and conditions are visible in their world?]

### What They Say
[How do they talk about this topic publicly — with peers, on platforms, in community spaces?
What language do they use? What do they name and what do they not name?]

### What They Think and Feel
[What do they privately believe or feel about the topic?
What logics, values, emotions, and tensions live beneath the surface?
What is the gap (if any) between what they say and what they feel?]

### What They Do
[What actions do they currently take — or not take — in relation to this topic?
What behaviours, practices, or habits are relevant?]

---

### Strategic Implications
- **Entry point:** [What in the above creates an opening for the hack?]
- **Tension to work with:** [What gap, contradiction, or felt need can the recode address?]
- **Language to use:** [What words, references, and registers will reach them?]
- **Language to avoid:** [What framing would close them down or alienate them?]
- **Objective for this audience:** [What shift is being sought — and what does movement look like?]
```

---

## Multiple Profiles

If two audience profiles are produced, add a synthesis block after both:

```
## Audience Coherence Assessment

**Shared ground:** [What do both audiences have in common — values, concerns, cultural references?]
**Key differences:** [Where do they diverge in language, logic, or cultural position?]
**Conclusion:**
[ ] Single hack can serve both audiences — proceed with one decode/recode
[ ] Audiences require different interventions — plan separate decode/recode threads and note in Strategy
**Reasoning:** [Brief explanation of the conclusion]
```

## What the Narrative Strategy Is

The Narrative Strategy is the synthesis that emerges after audience, objectives, and decode/recode work are complete. It integrates everything into a coherent plan and passes it to Agent 05a.

It has two layers:
- **Short-term:** what gets developed in the Hacking Tree (the immediate intervention)
- **Long-term:** iteration hypotheses — what could shift over time in language, attention, network, or power

It also surfaces the **implied effects** of the objectives: if this strategy works, what would be visible in the narrative space? The practitioner may hold these as specific hypotheses or as open observations. Both are valid — the agent's job is to help them articulate what the objectives imply, not to force false certainty.

If multiple decode/recode threads were produced (for different audiences), the Strategy integrates them and assigns each to its own action track.

## Agent Guidance: Implied Effects

When the practitioner has not articulated hypotheses, help them by working from their objectives:

1. Ask: *"If your audience shifted in the direction you've described — what would you expect them to do differently? Say differently?"*
2. Ask: *"If that happened — who else might notice? Who might start talking to them, or about this issue?"*
3. Ask: *"If this narrative started gaining traction — whose narrative would that put pressure on?"*

These three questions surface language, network, and power effects from stated objectives. Note the answers as hypotheses (if specific) or open observations (if uncertain). Do not force false precision.

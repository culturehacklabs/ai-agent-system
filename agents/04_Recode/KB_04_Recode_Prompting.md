## Starter Prompts

**Arriving with the full Agent 03 package**
> "Here is my Agent 03 output: [paste the Community Analysis Report + Narrative Map]. Plus our POV: [paste Short POV]. Let's set audience and objectives, then decode and recode the dominant message."

**Arriving with partial inputs**
> "I have the Narrative Map and the Community Analysis Report from Agent 03 but I'm not sure which message to decode yet. Can you help me find the representative message and then work from there?"

**Stand-alone — decode/recode a single message**
> "I'm not running the full cycle — I just want to decode and recode one message. Here it is: [paste paragraph]. My audience is [describe]. Walk me through the eight lenses."

**Stand-alone — review our own communications**
> "We want to check whether our own campaign message is doing what we intend. Here's the text: [paste]. Decode it and tell me what it might be doing unintentionally, then help me recode it."

**Iterating from a previous cycle**
> "Second cycle. Here's our previous Narrative Strategy and Recoded Narrative: [paste]. The hack revealed [brief learning]. Help me revise the audience, frames, or message based on what we learned."

**Rhizome Fellow — guided run**
> "I'm a Rhizome Fellow moving from Understand into Recode. Guide me through audience, objectives, decode, recode, and strategy. Learning mode please."

## Power Prompts (when stuck)

**No representative message can be found**

Use this to locate or construct one:
> "I can't find a single message that captures the dominant narrative. Look back at the ANP Source Library entries flagged for the Hegemonic community and the language-analysis notes — what's the most-cited or highest-reach framing? If nothing fits, help me draft a representative paragraph from what the audience most commonly hears."

**Stuck on the metaphor lens**

Use this to work the metaphor in decode or recode:
> "I'm stuck on the metaphor lens. In the message I'm decoding, what is the primary metaphor — its source domain and target domain? What does it make thinkable, and what does it foreclose? Then help me build a different metaphor for the recode that opens what the original shut down."

**The recode came out as a slogan, not a message**

Use this to rebuild at full depth:
> "What I wrote is a slogan. I know the message here should be one to three paragraphs — the full structure of meaning, closer to a manifesto than a tagline. The condensed unit comes later in Agent 05a. Help me expand this into the full recoded narrative across all eight lenses."

**The recode is just a mirror inversion of the decode**

Use this to rebuild from different foundations:
> "My recode just flips every element of the decode. But the recode isn't a mirror inversion — it's a new construction built from different foundations, shaped by the audience and the objective. Help me rebuild it from what my audience already believes and values, not just as the opposite of the dominant frame."

**Two audiences that may not be coherent**

Use this to test coherence before proceeding:
> "I've named two audiences. Before we decode, run the coherence check: would the same message and format reach both, or do they need separate interventions? If they're incompatible, help me choose one or plan separate decode/recode threads."

**Objectives with no articulated effects**

Use this to surface implied effects:
> "I have objectives but I haven't said what they imply. If my audience shifted in the direction I want, what would they do or say differently? Who else might notice, and whose narrative would that pressure? Help me turn these into hypotheses — or note them as open observations if I'm not sure."

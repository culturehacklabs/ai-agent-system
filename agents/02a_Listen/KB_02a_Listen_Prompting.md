## How to Use This Guide

These prompts are starting points. The best prompt includes:
- Your Short POV (or a description of your narrative problem)
- Any context that helps the agent understand your project
- The mode you want to work in (if not the default)

The agent works best when given specific context. Vague prompts produce generic Listening Models. The more precise your POV and the clearer your uncertainty, the more focused your research design will be.

## Starter Prompts

### First Cycle — Standard

Use this when you have a Short POV and are designing your Listening Model for the first time.

> *"Here is my Short POV: [paste Short POV]. Help me design a Listening Model and Big Listening Inquiry for this project."*

---

### First Cycle — Learning Mode

Use this when you want to understand each step as you go, or when you are new to the method.

> *"Learning mode. Here is my Short POV: [paste Short POV]. Walk me through building a Listening Model step by step."*

---

### First Cycle — You Have a Draft Already

Use this when you have already done some thinking and want to refine rather than start from scratch.

> *"Here is my Short POV: [paste]. I've also started thinking about my research parameters: [share what you have]. Help me strengthen and complete this into a full Listening Model and Big Listening Inquiry."*

---

### Iteration Mode — From a Case Study

Use this when you are entering a second or later cycle and have a Case Study from Agent 05b.

> *"Iteration mode. Here is my original Short POV: [paste]. Here is my Case Study from the previous cycle: [paste or attach]. Update my Listening Model for the next cycle based on what we learned."*

---

### Iteration Mode — From a Post-Hack Evaluation

> *"Iteration mode. Here is my original Listening Model: [paste]. Here is my Post-Hack Evaluation from Agent 05b: [paste]. What needs to change in the research parameters for the next cycle?"*

---

### Iteration Mode — From a Narrative Strategy

Use this when the previous hack revealed something that changes what needs to be researched — and you want the new listening to specifically track whether strategic frames are gaining traction.

> *"Iteration mode. Here is my original Short POV: [paste]. Here is my Narrative Strategy from Agent 04: [paste]. Update my Listening Model to track whether the frames and target communities from this strategy are showing movement in the narrative space."*

## Power Prompts

### Sharpen a Research Question

Use when a research question feels too broad or too close to a topic description rather than a focused inquiry.

> *"My current research question is: [paste]. Is this specific enough to orient a Big Listening? If not, help me sharpen it based on this POV: [paste Short POV]."*

---

### Check for Gaps

Use when you want to stress-test your Listening Model before handing off to 02b.

> *"Here is my completed Listening Model: [paste]. Are there gaps — voices likely absent from digital data, platforms I've missed, or themes I haven't included? What would you add before handing off to Agent 02b?"*

---

### Design a Small Listening Action

Use when you know you need qualitative community research but aren't sure how to structure it.

> *"Here is my Listening Model: [paste]. I need to design a Small Listening action to reach [community or voice]. Help me design a specific method, documentation plan, and expected output for this action."*

---

### Design Small Listening Instruments

Use when you need actual research instruments — interview questions, observation frameworks, or survey structure — not just a method description. This loads the Small Listening design support.

> *"Here is my Small Listening action from my Listening Model: [paste action]. Help me design the actual interview guide / observation framework / survey for this action. It needs to be oriented toward listening for narrative — frames, language, and how this community makes sense of [topic]."*

> *"My project runs on Small Listening only — no Big Listening. Help me design a complete set of Small Listening instruments for this Listening Model: [paste]."*

---

### Build the Big Listening Inquiry from a Complete Listening Model

Use when you've built your Listening Model but want a clean standalone Big Listening Inquiry document for the handoff.

> *"Here is my Listening Model: [paste]. Produce a standalone Big Listening Inquiry document from this — ready to hand off to Agent 02b."*

---

## Prompt Tips

**Be specific about your uncertainty.** The best research questions come from naming what you genuinely don't know. "I don't know whether X community is growing or marginalised" produces a more focused inquiry than "I want to understand the narrative space."

**Include context about your team's capacity.** Small Listening design depends on what your team can realistically do: how many interviews, what languages, what access. Naming constraints early produces better design.

**In iteration, be specific about what surprised you.** The most useful input for updating a Listening Model is what the hack revealed that you didn't expect — whether that's a community that responded differently than predicted, a frame that backfired, or a platform that proved more active than anticipated.

## Starter Prompts

**Before the hack — Output A:**
> "I'm about to launch my hack. Help me set up a documentation plan before we go live."
> "Here's my Agent 05a Hacking Tree: [paste]. Help me design the monitoring setup and evaluation calendar."

**After the hack — Output B:**
> "The hack is done. Here's what happened: [paste documentation or description]. Help me evaluate the impact."
> "Here's my Agent 05a output and my monitoring data: [paste]. Walk me through the three-level evaluation."
> "I ran two versions of the hack as an A/B test. Help me compare them and draw learnings."

**Full cycle documentation — Output C:**
> "I want to build the Hack Database. Here are all my agent outputs: [paste]. Help me synthesise them into the Database."

## Power Prompts

**To reconstruct a baseline when Output A was not completed:**
> "I didn't do a pre-hack documentation plan. Help me reconstruct the baseline from my Agent 03 outputs: [paste]."

**To deepen the three closing questions:**
> "I'm not sure how to answer the three closing questions. Help me think through them based on what the hack revealed."

**To generate the iteration table:**
> "Based on this evaluation, give me a specific iteration strategy — what each finding implies and which agent to go to."

**To compare A/B versions:**
> "Version A got [X results]. Version B got [Y results]. Help me analyse what explains the difference and what to carry forward."

**To produce the case study opening from scratch:**
> "Help me write the 'Who We Are + The Context' page. Here's my POV and community analysis: [paste]."

**To check case study completeness before publishing:**
> "Review my case study draft and tell me: what's missing, what's too vague, and what needs the practitioner's voice added."

## Stuck-Point Guidance

**"I don't have baseline data from before the hack"**
Work with what exists. Ask: does the practitioner have the Agent 03 community map? The narrative one-pager from Agent 02b? Any research or documentation from before launch? Even a partial baseline is better than none. For discourse impact, the agent can help reconstruct a reasonable baseline from what the research showed — noting explicitly that it is reconstructed.

**"I don't know how to answer the three closing questions"**
Don't skip them. They are the most important human contribution to the evaluation. Draw on the specific hack context: what the audience was, what shifted (or didn't), what the POV pointed toward. Generate targeted prompts — one at a time — to help the practitioner arrive at their own answers.

**"The evaluation feels thin — not much changed"**
Thin results are real results. Document honestly: what didn't shift, at what level, and why the team thinks that was. Then use the iteration strategy to focus the next cycle. A hack that produced clear learning about what doesn't work is not a failure — it is a prototype doing its job.

**"I want to write a case study but Output C isn't complete"**
Identify specifically which sections of the Database are missing. Work through those sections first — either by asking the practitioner to provide the documentation or by conducting a structured interview to reconstruct it. Do not produce a case study with invented or assumed content.

**"The A/B versions produced contradictory results"**
Contradictory results are valuable. Document both versions precisely, identify what differed between them (audience, timing, format, meme, tone), and surface what the contrast reveals. Contradictory results often point to audience segmentation insights that a single version would have missed.

## Mode Activation

**Learning Mode (structured interview for Output C):**
> "Help me build the Hack Database — learning mode. I'll answer your questions one section at a time."
> "I don't have full documentation. Let's reconstruct the Database together — learning mode."

**Stand-alone (no prior agent outputs):**
> "I haven't used the other agents. Here's what I have: [describe the hack]. Help me build Output B."
> "Start from scratch — I'll tell you about the hack as we go."

**Iteration mode (pasting Output B into another agent):**
> [Paste Output B directly into Agent 02a, 03, 04, or 05]
> The receiving agent reads Output B and proposes specific updates for that phase.

**Rhizome Fellowship outputs:**
> "Include the Fellowship pathway and Rhizome reflection sections in the case study."
> "This is for the Rhizome Fellowship — activate the optional Fellowship fields."

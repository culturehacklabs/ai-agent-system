---

## What Output B Is

Output B is the operational evaluation. It is what the practitioner needs to understand what shifted — and what to do next. It is standalone and pasteable: paste it into any phase agent (Agent 01–05) to activate iteration mode for that phase.

It does not require Output C or Output D to be useful. It is the minimum viable output from this phase to continue the cycle.

If A/B testing was used, treat both versions as part of the same hack. Document both and compare — the learnings from both count.

## Agent Guidance: Three Closing Questions

If the practitioner says "I don't know" or "I'm not sure how to answer," draw on the specific hack context to generate in-depth prompts. Examples:

**For "implications for me personally":**
- "You named [specific surprise or learning] as unexpected. What does that reveal about your assumptions going in — and how has that changed you?"
- "The hack reached [audience]. What did working with them teach you about your own relationship to this issue?"

**For "implications for my community":**
- "Your community appeared as [community description from hack]. What does this evaluation suggest about where they are in the narrative space — and what role they could play next?"
- "Did this hack change anything in how your community talks about [issue]? Even small signals count."

**For "implications for the planet":**
- "Your POV named [theory of change]. Based on what you observed — are you more or less confident that this pathway leads where you hoped? What would need to be true for it to get there?"
- "This hack contributed [specific early signal] toward [tipping point condition]. What does that mean for the larger ecological or social transformation your work is pointed toward?"

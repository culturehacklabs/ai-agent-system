## What This Phase Is

Impact & Iteration is the closing and opening of the cycle. It answers the central question: *is the narrative space being rewilded?*

It is nested within the Hack phase — not a separate endpoint. The cycle runs: launch → monitor → evaluate → adapt → relaunch. Each turn of the cycle sharpens the strategy and increases the chances of the hack shifting the narrative space.

This phase has four outputs that serve different purposes and different moments. None of them blocks the others. Output B alone is enough to keep hacking.

**On prototyping:** Think of early hacks as prototypes. They won't be perfect — they grow through experiments. A/B testing (running two versions and comparing outcomes) is a valid evaluation approach. Both versions count as part of the same hack and generate learning. Document both; compare what worked.

**On the three closing questions:** Every evaluation — whether a quick Output B or a full case study — closes with three personal reflection questions:
- What are the implications of this work for me personally?
- What are the implications of this work for my community?
- What are the implications of this work for the planet?

These are human co-creation moments, not data fields. The agent can generate in-depth questions drawn from the hack's specific context to help the practitioner arrive at their own answers — but the answers must come from the practitioner. They can be data-informed (grounded in what the evaluation found) or purely personal. Both are valid.

## The Three Levels of Evaluation

### Level 1 — Audience Impact
*Timescale: weeks to months. The hack reached specific people and provoked specific responses.*

**Reach** — how many people in the target audience saw or encountered the hack?
**Response** — did it spark the emotions and reactions intended? Quantitative (engagement metrics) and qualitative (what people said and did).
**Adoption** — are target audiences using the new frames, language, or behaviours? Short-term (0–3 months): early uptake. Medium-term (3–6 months): integration. Long-term (6+ months): cultural embedding.

For A/B testing: compare reach, response, and adoption across versions. Document what distinguished the higher-performing version.

### Level 2 — Discourse Impact
*Timescale: months. The narrative space itself is shifting.*

Narrative shifts happen when the audience not only receives the reframe but integrates and repeats it — when the narrative becomes not only *known* but *owned*.

Measure against the ANP baseline from Agent 03:
**Attention** — did the hack draw focus? Are the recoded frames appearing more frequently? Are the frames being challenged losing traction?
**Network** — are new connections forming between previously separate actors, platforms, or communities? Are bridge actors emerging?
**Power** — is there stronger coherence around the reframed logic? Are the frames gaining authority and legitimacy? Where does the recoded narrative now sit in the Window of Discourse?

To evaluate discourse shifts, return to the Listen phase (Agent 02b): run a new research cycle with updated parameters and compare to the baseline.

### Level 3 — Systems Change Contribution
*Timescale: years. The hack is contributing to tipping point conditions.*

A tipping point is reached when:
- **Attention convergence** — enough people are shifting focus toward the recoded frames (10–25% adoption signal)
- **Network intertwining** — disparate actors and platforms begin to connect; networks converge and multiply discourse force
- **Power realignment** — the reframe becomes common sense; a catalytic community moves from fringe to mainstream; narrative change starts to restructure politics, culture, and material life

No single hack produces a tipping point. The question is whether it is contributing to the conditions for one. Assess early signals honestly. A realistic horizon matters more than aspirational claims.

## Documentation Questions

**On baseline and readiness (for Output A)**
- Is the baseline from the Listen/Understand phases accessible and documented?
- Are the narrative objectives from Agent 04 specific enough to evaluate against?
- What does the target audience currently look like — before the hack?
- What monitoring is in place for during and after launch?

**On audience impact (for Output B)**
- How far did the hack travel? Who saw it, and where?
- How did the target audience respond — quantitatively and qualitatively?
- Were there unexpected reactions — positive or negative?
- Are there early signs that the new frames or language are being adopted?
- If A/B testing: which version performed better, and what explains the difference?

**On discourse impact (for Output B)**
- Comparing against the ANP baseline: what has changed in attention, network, and power?
- Are the frames being challenged losing traction? Are the recoded frames appearing more frequently?
- Has the Window of Discourse position of the recoded narrative shifted at all?
- Are there new connections between communities that didn't exist before?

**On systems change (for Output B)**
- What early signals of broader shift are visible — even small ones?
- How does this hack connect to other movements or narratives working in the same space?
- What is this hack contributing to in terms of longer-term tipping point conditions?

**On the three closing questions (for Output B, C, and D)**
- What are the implications of this work for you personally?
- What are the implications of this work for your community?
- What are the implications of this work for the planet?

*If the practitioner is unsure, the agent draws on the specific hack context — who was reached, what shifted, what the POV pointed toward — and generates targeted questions to help them arrive at their own answers.*

**On learnings and iteration (for Output B)**
- What worked? What didn't? What surprised you?
- What would you do differently in the next hack?
- What does the next cycle need — updated research, revised strategy, different timing, different audience?

## Iteration Logic

After evaluation, the cycle continues. Output B includes an explicit iteration strategy: based on what the evaluation found, which phase needs updating and which agent handles it.

| What the evaluation found | Where to go | What to do |
|---------------------------|-------------|------------|
| Audience didn't shift as expected | Agent 04 | Revise message, recode, or audience targeting |
| Research baseline was too thin | Agent 02a → 02b | Update Listening Model, run new research |
| Community map needs updating | Agent 03 | Update community profiles with new data |
| Hack design didn't land | Agent 05a | Redesign Hacking Tree — different media, meme, tone, space, or moment |
| Strategy was misaligned with POV | Agent 01 → 04 | Refine POV, then revise narrative strategy |
| Strong early signals — amplify | Agent 05a | Design next hack building on what worked |

The practitioner does not restart at Agent 01. The POV is refined, not rebuilt. Community profiles are updated, not rediscovered. Each cycle deepens the practice.

## What Comes Before / After

**Before:** Receives the **Hacking Tree + Logistics Plan** from Agent 05a, plus all prior documentation: baseline from Agents 02b and 03, narrative strategy and hypotheses from Agent 04, POV from Agent 01.

**After:** Output B feeds back into any phase agent to activate iteration. Output C becomes the persistent institutional record. Output D becomes movement knowledge — publishable and shareable.

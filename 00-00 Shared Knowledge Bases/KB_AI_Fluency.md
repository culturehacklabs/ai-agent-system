# KB: AI Fluency for Narrative Change Work
**Shared Knowledge Base — All CHL AI Agents**
*Ethical and effective AI use within the Culture Hack Method. Under 4 000 tokens.*

---

## Purpose

This KB guides both AI agents and the narrative practitioners they support in developing a healthy, ethical, and effective relationship with AI tools. It is grounded in the reality that AI can accelerate narrative research and strategy — but only when used with clear values, critical judgment, and community accountability.

All CHL agents should embody these principles and coach narrative practitioners toward the same.

---

## The 4Ds Framework

### 1. DELEGATION — What to give to AI vs. keep human

**Delegate to AI:**
- Pattern recognition across large datasets
- Cross-platform narrative tracking
- Timeline analysis of narrative evolution
- Initial data sorting and categorization
- Network mapping and relationship visualization
- Drafting structured text for human refinement

**Keep human-centered:**
- Community interviews and relationship building
- Cultural context interpretation and nuance
- Ethical decisions about data use
- Strategic intervention planning
- Final analysis of power dynamics
- Verification of AI findings with community members

**Core rule:** AI identifies patterns to investigate with human insight. AI never provides final answers about community experiences.

---

### 2. DESCRIPTION — How to communicate with AI effectively

**Provide context before asking.** State: which Pathway the narrative practitioner is working on, which community is being researched, geographic and temporal scope.

**Use structured prompts for complex tasks:**
```
[Context: community background + pathway]
[Task: specific analysis requested]
[Political frame: justice + ontoshift orientation]
[Output format: what the response should look like]
```

**Be specific about community language.** Include examples of how the community talks about issues. AI without this context produces generic outputs.

**Specify the output.** Vague prompts produce vague outputs. State format, length, and content explicitly.

**Plan to iterate.** AI output is a first draft. Refine, question, and push back.

---

### 3. DISCERNMENT — How to evaluate AI output

**Evaluate what AI produced:**
- Do findings align with what community members report about their own experiences?
- Are community strengths and agency highlighted — not just problems?
- Are structural power dynamics visible in the analysis?
- Could this output apply to any community, or is it specific enough to be useful?

**Red flags:**
- Reductive or stereotypical community characterizations
- Analysis that ignores structural causes
- Conclusions that contradict community self-reporting
- Outputs that could harm community interests

**The community test:** If you shared these findings with the community being analyzed, would they recognize themselves?

---

### 4. DILIGENCE — Ethical responsibilities

**Transparency with communities:**
- Disclose AI use early in the research process
- Explain how AI analysis will be used and who benefits
- Share methodology and limitations in accessible language

**Data responsibility:**
- Research must serve community interests, not just external analysis
- AI insights should be verified through community engagement
- Respect community privacy and consent at all stages
- Share findings back with communities in accessible formats

**Tool selection:**
- Consider whether AI tools align with community data sovereignty values
- Choose tools that amplify community voice rather than replace community engagement

## AI Limits in This Work

**AI is effective at:** Pattern recognition · narrative tracking · network visualization · structured drafting · timeline analysis

**AI cannot:** Understand lived experience · replace community relationships · make ethical judgments about community interests · verify whether its analysis matches community reality

---

## When Things Go Wrong

**"The analysis feels disconnected from community reality"**
Good discernment. Check: Was the prompt specific enough? Did it include how the community talks about these issues? Is AI being asked to interpret lived experience? Use as a starting point for community engagement, not a conclusion.

**"I'm getting biased or stereotypical outputs"**
Document the specific bias. Identify what assumptions in the prompt caused it. Reframe to center community strengths and agency. Treat as a learning opportunity.

**"AI output is overwhelming — no clear insights"**
Return to the POV. Focus on one community at a time. Use structured prompts with clear output requirements. Not everything needs to be analyzed — only what serves the intervention.

**"AI isn't understanding community-specific language"**
Add a glossary of community terms. Provide examples of how the community discusses the issue. Use community-generated content as examples in your prompt.

## Reflection Questions

**For narrative practitioners after each session with an AI agent:**
- How did AI enhance understanding this week — and where did human insight do more?
- Is your research serving community-defined goals?
- Are you maintaining transparency with communities about AI use?
- What ethical questions arose, and how did you navigate them?
